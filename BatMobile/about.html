About BatMobile

BatMobile is a Jisc funded Nature Locator (University of Bristol) pilot project to investigate the feasibility of producing a smartphone app capable of automatically identifying European bat species from their calls. Both the Zoological Society of London and the Bat Conservation Trust were partners on the project.

The study uses call recognition code from the open source project Bat BioAcoustics and demonstrated that the technology is capable of processing the data and determining bat ID to genus or species level (dependent on the species in question* and the quality of the detection algorithm).

*Some bat species have such similar calls that separation via calls alone is rarely possible.

Stage two of the project would seek to include the audio capture element into the workflow and increase the accuracy of the bat identification algorithms, whilst refining the user interface.

About Nature Locator

Nature Locator is a University of Bristol project which produces smartphone app solutions for researchers and companies looking to crowd-source data collection for ecology based projects.

Projects to date include:

  • Conker Tree Science: LeafWatch
  • PlantTracker
  • Sealife Tracker
  • iRecord Ladybirds
  • iRecord Butterflies
  • BatMobile
  • RINSE
  • AquaInvaders

For further information on these projects please visit naturelocator.org
